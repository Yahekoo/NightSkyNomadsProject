About the WebSite

Solar eclipses answer fundamental questions about how heat and energy are transferred from the Sun out into the solar wind, the continuous stream of particles that the Sun spews into the solar system, requires research into the innermost region of the corona, the Sun's outer atmosphere, which is only visible during total solar eclipses. Understanding how the solar wind is accelerated at the Sun can help predict how it will affect technologies on Earth and humans in space. Total solar eclipses offer a unique chance to learn more about Earth's atmosphere. In contrast to the daily global shift in light at dusk and dawn, a solar eclipse alters the illumination of Earth and its atmosphere over a very narrow zone that is partially or completely obscured.
